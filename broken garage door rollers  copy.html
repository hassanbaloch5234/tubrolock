Signs Your Roller Garage Doors Need to be Repaired or Replaced

Damaged or broken rollers will make it very difficult to operate your garage door. In fact, it might become virtually impossible to open and close it. Before it gets to that point, however, there are signs that could indicate your garage door rollers are worn out: Clicking sounds. If you hear a clicking sound when you open or close your garage door, it’s very likely that the rollers are damaged and need to be replaced. The clicking sound is the result of the machinery being misaligned and not moving properly on its track. Dragging motion. The springs on your overhead garage door carry a log of tension, which makes it easy to open and close the door. If the spring is in good condition but you are feeling a dragging-like resistance when you’re opening and closing the door, the rollers may be damaged. Grinding sounds. If you notice anything that sounds like metal rubbing together, chances are that your garage door rollers are damaged. When the rollers are in need of repair or have to be replaced, the metal components of an overhead roll-up door will rub together, creating a grinding noise. If you notice any of these signs, you need to contact a reputable garage door repair company as soon as possible. The longer you want to have your overhead roll-up door inspected and repaired, the worse the damage will become.

Why Do Garage Door Rollers Become Damaged?

There are several reasons why the rollers on overhead garage doors can become damaged. Some of the most common reasons include: Not enough lubrication. All moving parts of a garage door, including the track and rollers, need to be well lubricated to ensure they are moving smoothly. Lack of lubrication can damage the rollers. Weather conditions. Excessively hot or cold weather, as well as humidity, can damage garage door rollers. This is a problem that tends to happen in areas where there’s a lot of salt in the air, as it can accelerate the oxidation of the metal. Garage door rollers are small wheels that run inside the tracks on either side of your garage door, allowing it to open and close smoothly.

Rollers are crucial for the smooth and safe operation of your garage door. If they're broken or malfunctioning, your door might not open or close properly, which could pose a security risk and increase wear on other door components.

Common signs include: a noisy garage door when operating, the door not opening or closing smoothly, or visibly damaged or out-of-track rollers.

Rollers can wear out from regular use, lack of maintenance, exposure to the elements, or misalignment of the garage door tracks. Sometimes, debris in the tracks can also hinder roller movement.

Ideally, inspect your rollers at least twice a year. Most rollers have a lifespan of about 10,000 cycles. If you use your garage door 4 times a day, that's roughly 7 years. However, conditions vary, so always be proactive in checking.

While it's possib le for homeowners with DIY experience to replace rollers, it's recommended to consult or hire professionals. Garage doors are heavy, and incorrect handling can lead to injuries or further damage.
