If your garage door wheel has come off track, you can typically reposition it by following these steps: Disconnect the power to the opener to prevent accidental activation. Carefully lift the bottom of the door and slide the wheel back onto the track, ensuring it is properly seated in the track groove. Check the alignment of the track and adjust as needed to ensure it is straight and parallel. Test the door to ensure the wheel stays on track and moves smoothly, and adjust the tension on the springs if necessary. If the wheel continues to come off track or if there are any signs of damage to the tracks or rollers, it's best to consult with a professional technician to inspect and repair the door properly.

While it may be tempting to replace only one garage door spring to save money, it's generally recommended to replace both springs at the same time, even if only one is broken. Garage door springs typically have a similar lifespan, and replacing only one spring can lead to uneven wear and imbalance, causing strain on the opener and other components. Additionally, replacing both springs ensures consistent performance and reduces the risk of future issues or failures.

Attempting to open a garage door with a broken spring can be extremely dangerous and should be avoided. Torsion springs are responsible for counterbalancing the weight of the door, and a broken spring can cause the door to become unbalanced and fall unexpectedly, potentially causing serious injury or property damage. If you suspect your garage door spring is broken, it's best to refrain from using the door and contact a professional technician to inspect and repair the spring safely.
